ABOUT ME
My name is Tara Nez. I’m born and raised on the Navajo reservation in Northern Arizona, where I grew to love and honor my cultural ties to the land and my people. My passion in architecture began when I was a little girl and my father would come home with blueprints of houses (he was a carpenter/ construction manager). I would constantly ask questions of what things were and I even had the chance to build an architect desk with him. Shortly after that my dad passed and I wanted to pursue architecture, not only to learn but to also honor his memory. I decided to pursue a degree in Architecture at Arizona State University where I got my Bachelors in Science and Design. After receiving my architectural degree, I interned at Urban Rural Design in Flagstaff, AZ. This is where I have fostered new skills such as BIM modeler, drafting, project managing, drone piloting, virtual reality, surveying, and working one on one with clients.
